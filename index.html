Description

Tigers are the largest felines in the world.
Most have the known orange fur with brown
or black stripes but specific looks vary between
subspecies.Their vertical stripes are most
likely an evolutionary advantage when it comes
to camouflage in their environments.
It is a mammal and a carnivore.

Traits

Tigers usually feed on medium to large sized
prey which includes deer and wild boar.
They will also hunt smaller prey given the
chance monkeys and ground based birds depending
on the location. If in the range of humans,
they will also prey on livestock.

Behavior

Tigers are usually very solitary animals. But they
can occasionally share their prey with another tiger
if the situation comes to it. Tigers are also quite
solitary up until it is time to mate. But after breeding,
tigers split up again. This entails the female being the
primary caregiver for tiger cubs.
